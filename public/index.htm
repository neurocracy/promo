Neurocracy

Solve a murder case in a near future world by diving into the Wikipedia of that future

Features

A world at your fingertips

Have you ever lost yourself in a Wikipedia rabbit hole? Imagine doing that for the Wikipedia of the year 2049. This is Neurocracy, an interactive narrative experience that tells a compelling sci-fi story inside and between the hyperlinked articles of an online encyclopedia. It is both a single-player alternate reality game and an epistolary hypertext novel, opening with a high-profile assassination that you investigate.

A world that builds over time

The story of Neurocracy is episodic and unfolds across ten days in the year 2049. Each episode represents a snapshot of the events of a single day, with new articles uploaded and existing ones updated to simulate bouts of frantic editing that reflect the global fallout of the assassination. Each article offers a different narrative thread to follow, and you can even dive in randomly by clicking Random article.

A world as realistic as it gets

Instead of bending the science to fit the fiction, Neurocracy uses accurate science and sociopolitics to craft its world, which exists at the intersection of surveillance capitalism, consumer-grade neurotech, and pandemic control. To build that world on top of our own, we're looking at the science of artificial neural networks, implanted neural devices, and neurodegenerative prion diseases. That might be why it's called Neurocracy.
